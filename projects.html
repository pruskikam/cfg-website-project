    Oasia hotel, Singapore

    27-floor external facade is wrapped in a natural vine covered sunscreen. It has 1,793 large planter boxes, and four large structural cores that allow for good cross ventilation reducing the overall energy cost. Designed by WOHA and completed in 2016, the hotel is home to over 33 species of plants and 21 species of creepers.

    Silo Restaurant, London

    The restaurant’s interiors reflect 'zero-waste' ideology and Nina+Co created a gorgeous space which has Mycellium pendant lights hanging over a Mycellium chair with a table made from recycled plastic and sustainably sourced wood. Guests can enjoy a cocktail at the fluted bar counter which is made from recycled plastic packaging. The wall lamps are made by a local artisan from recycled wine bottles, netted sheeps wool lines the ceiling and cork tile flooring is used throughout.

    Moar Gut, a Family Nature Resort in Großarl, Austria

    All natural childcare centre has been designed without any plastic. Designers used a plethora of sustainable solutions, such as LED lighting, alpine grasses and herbs used as wallpaper and fall protection mats and covers made from coconut mats, natural rubber and linen.

    Earthship, Brighton

    It is a self-sustainable building, completed in 2006 and owned by the Low Carbon Trust, situated in Stanmer Park, Brighton.

    The building is an example of passive solar earth sheltered design and was constructed using waste car tyres and other recycled materials, such as cans and bottles. It uses the planet’s natural systems to provide heat, power and water and is designed to work as an autonomous building. Earthship Brighton is currently one of only two Earthships in the UK, and was the first Earthship to be built in England.
