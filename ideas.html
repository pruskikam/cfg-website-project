    • Ideas for every room

    bathroom

    • install automatic faucets
    • if possible, install at home low flow fixtures - they can save up to 60% of water, compared to standard fixtures
    • install a system that uses greywater
    • rimless bowls are a great invention - with no rim, the toilet is more hygienic and far easier to clean, meaning you’re using less water and less harmful detergents
    • check out strawboard cabinets are now also increasing in popularity as an eco-friendly alternative to medium density fibre (MDF)
    • for tiles, pick clay, stone or tiles from recycled materials

    kitchen

    • a good material to consider is plywood - it is durable, hard-wearing and sustainable. As it is manufactured with minimal chemicals compared to materials like MDF or chipboard, there is little or no VOCs involved, contributing to a toxin-free home.
    • upcycle old furniture, surfaces and textiles - it is not only sustaianble, but also adds more personal touch to the space
    • typical paint features microplastics as their binding agent - if possible, use eco paints
    • installing modern appliances might help you save not only the planet, but your energy bill too! A boiling water tap, for example, not only provides hot water on demand, but also aims to tackle the £68 million a year the UK wastes in electricity boiling kettles
    • swap any incandescent bulbs for LEDs, which are 75-80% more efficient. They also last 25 times longer!

    bedroom

    • if you need new curtains, look for natural materials such as bamboo linen, GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard) certified organic cotton, hemp etc.
    • wherever possible buy secondhand furniture!
    • an eco-friendly mattress should be made of natural and non-toxic materials. Materials such as natural latex, certified organic cotton, organic wool and organic bamboo and hemp. Flame retardants can also be toxic so check those out as well.
    • and of course, use house plants as room decorations!
    • prevent heat loss and reduce energy use, by checking window for draughts and by using heavy curtains
